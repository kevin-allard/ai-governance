Carnegie Learning’s Approach to AI

At Carnegie Learning, we believe AI should be a responsible, human-centered partner in education. Our AI-enabled products, services, and content development practices are designed to empower students, support educators, and enhance instruction through ethical, transparent, and pedagogy-driven use of technology.

Our approach prioritizes student success, safeguards data privacy, respects intellectual property, and maintains the critical role of educators, content experts, and human judgment in the learning process. We use AI to strengthen teaching and learning, not to replace the expertise, care, and decision-making that high-quality education requires.

Our guiding principles

Our guiding principles shape how we use AI in our products, services, and content development processes.

Elevating Educators, Not Replacing Them

Recognizing the central role of teachers in education, we use AI to elevate that role by helping reduce administrative burden, supporting instructional insight, and enabling educators to focus more time on student learning. AI may inform and support instructional decisions, but educators remain central to the learning experience.

Ethical, Transparent, and Equitable AI

We design and review AI-enabled products and content with attention to fairness, accessibility, representation, and bias. When users interact directly with generative AI based agents or features, we clearly identify that experience. When AI materially contributes to customer-facing products, content, or student experiences, we maintain governance processes that allow us to explain the role AI played and the human oversight applied.

Pedagogy-Driven and Coherent

We develop AI solutions that align with evidence-based instructional practices, learning science, and Carnegie Learning’s standards for academic quality. AI is used to support student thinking, problem-solving, critical thinking, and active learning. Efficiency and automation must not come at the expense of instructional coherence, rigor, or assessment validity.

Purposeful Personalization

Where AI is used to support personalization, recommendations, or student-facing experiences, we apply safeguards appropriate to the use case. Personalized learning should preserve student agency, support collaboration, and remain grounded in sound pedagogy. AI-supported recommendations or experiences must be designed to enhance, not narrow, the role of teachers and students in the learning process.

Human Oversight and Accountability

AI may assist in content creation, instructional support, analysis, and workflow improvement, but human expertise remains central. For high-stakes instructional, assessment, scoring, placement, or student-impact use cases, AI-supported outputs are subject to appropriate human review, validation, and approval. Carnegie Learning maintains accountability for the quality, accuracy, safety, and integrity of AI-assisted work.

Data Privacy and Responsible Stewardship

We do not permit third-party generative AI providers to train on our student data. When training our own models, we use aggregate data that does not contain personally identifiable information (PII). Student interaction data is handled in compliance with FERPA, COPPA, contractual obligations, and our internal privacy and security standards. Sensitive, confidential, or regulated data may only be used in approved environments.

Respect for Intellectual Property and Human Authorship

When AI supports product or content development, Carnegie Learning requires meaningful human authorship, expert judgment, and accountability for final deliverables. AI may assist with drafting, refinement, accessibility, localization, quality assurance, and other workflow support, but it does not replace human creative control or instructional responsibility. Our vendors and partners must also follow Carnegie Learning’s AI governance requirements, including restrictions on unapproved AI use and prohibitions against using Carnegie Learning content, prompts, student data, or deliverables to train external AI models without explicit approval.

Responsible AI Use by Our Teams

We use AI as a force multiplier for our teams, not as a replacement for human expertise, judgment, or accountability. Carnegie Learning employees and partners are responsible for verifying AI-assisted work, protecting sensitive information, and ensuring that deliverables meet our standards for quality, accuracy, accessibility, and integrity.

Our commitment

Our AI governance process includes review, documentation, release controls, and ongoing monitoring so that AI use in customer-facing products and content remains transparent, accountable, and auditable. As AI capabilities evolve, we will continue to refine our practices while staying grounded in our mission: helping students learn, supporting educators, and delivering high-quality instructional experiences built on trust.

For more information about how we protect customer data, visit our Security Practices, Privacy Policy, and Terms of Use.
